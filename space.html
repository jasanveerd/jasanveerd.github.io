Space Engineering

My interest in space engineering focuses on building robotic systems that can operate reliably in extreme extraterrestrial environments where failure is not an option.

Why Space?

My curiosity about space began in middle school when my teacher talked about the Cassini mission and shared updates on its discoveries. Learning about a spacecraft exploring another planet made space feel vast, unknown, and exciting.

During high school, watching SpaceX’s Demo-2 launch during the pandemic completely captivated me. Seeing astronauts launch from Earth for the first time in nearly a decade showed how far human innovation has come—and how much farther we can still go.

Motor Control & Programming

Space robotics relies heavily on precise motor control and robust programming. Systems must execute accurate movements while adapting to delays, uncertainty, and limited communication with Earth.

Harsh Environments

Space presents extreme challenges such as radiation exposure, vacuum conditions, and massive temperature swings. Engineering solutions must prioritize reliability, redundancy, and material durability.

Space System Design

Designing space systems requires integrating mechanical, electrical, and software components into a unified platform while meeting strict constraints on mass, power consumption, and safety.

Hardware for Robots

Robotic space missions depend on high-reliability hardware including sensors, actuators, controllers, and power systems that allow autonomous operation in environments where human intervention is impossible.
